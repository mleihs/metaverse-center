# The Devouring Mother — Resonance Dungeon Design Document

## 1. Literary Foundation

### 1.1 Primary Influences

**Jeff VanderMeer — "Annihilation" / Southern Reach Trilogy (2014)**

Area X is the Devouring Mother as landscape. It does not destroy the expeditions that enter it — it *incorporates* them. The biologist's transformation is not death but assimilation into something larger, older, more alive. VanderMeer's crucial insight: Area X is beautiful. The horror is not that it kills you but that you cannot resist wanting to become part of it. The transformations produce wonders. The tower writes in living text. The environment doesn't just exist — it *does things to you*, and those things feel like improvement.

For the dungeon: the environment is not hostile. It is generous. It provides warmth, healing, beauty. The corridors are alive and they are alive *for you*. The organic growth on the walls is not disease — it is the dungeon extending itself toward the party. Each room is more accommodating than the last. This is the horror.

**Octavia Butler — "Bloodchild" (1984) / Xenogenesis Trilogy (1987-89)**

Butler's Tlic lay eggs inside human hosts. This is parasitism. But the Tlic also provide protection, longevity, and genuine affection. Butler refuses to resolve the ambiguity: is this love or exploitation? Both. Always both. The hosts "pay the rent" — survival costs the body. The Oankali of Xenogenesis offer humanity rescue from extinction, but the price is genetic assimilation. Species identity dissolves into something new. Consent becomes meaningless when the alternative is death.

For the dungeon: the party needs the Mother. Her healing is real. Her protection is genuine. The parasitic drain is the rent they pay for surviving her domain. Every gift is simultaneously sustenance and consumption. The party cannot refuse the Mother's care without dying. They cannot accept it without being consumed. This is Butler's lesson, and it is the Devouring Mother's core.

**Shirley Jackson — "We Have Always Lived in the Castle" (1962)**

The Blackwood house protects Merricat and Constance from a hostile world. It is sanctuary. It is also prison. But Jackson's genius is that Merricat loves the prison. The house-as-mother is not an external force — it is an internalized condition. "We eat the foods we always eat." Ritual, routine, the domestic cycle — these are the walls. The Devouring Mother does not need physical barriers. She only needs the victim to prefer inside to outside.

For the dungeon: as parasitic attachment rises, the dungeon becomes *comfortable*. The party begins to feel that remaining is preferable to leaving. Banter shifts from clinical observation to warmth. Rest sites become more effective. The deeper the party goes, the more the dungeon provides. The exit is always available. The party simply stops wanting to use it.

**Samanta Schweblin — "Fever Dream" / "Distancia de rescate" (2014)**

Schweblin's "rescue distance" — the maternal leash, the invisible tether between protector and protected. The novel's horror: the rescue distance fails. Environmental contamination poisons the child despite maternal vigilance. The Devouring Mother shortens the rescue distance to zero. Protection becomes incorporation. The Mother cannot protect the child from the world, so she absorbs the child into herself. If there is no distance, there can be no danger.

For the dungeon: the environment responds to the party's needs. It notices them. It adjusts. The rescue distance between the party and the dungeon shrinks with each room entered. At high attachment, the dungeon IS the party's immune system. Leaving means losing that immunity.

### 1.2 Tone and Style

**Tenderness that suffocates. Abundance that annihilates. Beauty that consumes.**

The Shadow uses *absence as presence* (the darkness is intentional). The Tower uses *architecture as debt* (the building keeps a ledger). The Entropy uses *sameness as dissolution* (things do not break; they become indistinguishable). The Devouring Mother uses *care as consumption* (the environment provides, and the provision is the threat).

The emotional register is a lullaby sung by something that doesn't sleep: maternal, patient, warm. The warmth increases. It never stops increasing. The party's instruments read "biological threat" while their bodies read "home."

### 1.3 Key Vocabulary

**Primary:** grow, feed, sustain, embrace, swell, pulse, warm, root, spore, tissue, membrane, nurture, provide, depend, cling, thicken, ripen, bloom, attach, offer

**Secondary:** consume, digest, absorb, incorporate, symbiont, host, attach, colonize, permeate, yield, cradle, smother, gestate

**Forbidden (owned by other archetypes):**
- shatter, explode (too violent/active)
- collapse, crumble (Tower's words)
- decay, dissolve, erode, fade (Entropy's words)
- shadow, darkness, absence (Shadow's domain)
- "devour" itself (too on-the-nose — show, don't tell)

### 1.4 Writing Technique

Start with something beautiful. End with the realization that the beauty is the threat.

> "The corridor is warm. The walls are soft to the touch — not damp, but yielding. Like skin that expects your hand."

Start with a provision. End with the cost.

> "{agent} rests. The air itself seems to sustain — each breath carries nutrients the body didn't know it needed. The parasitic attachment counter ticks upward."

### 1.5 S-Tier Reference Sentences

These establish the literary standard. All Devouring Mother banter must meet this bar:

- "The walls pulse. Gently. Like something breathing for you so you don't have to."
- "{agent} drinks from the pool. The water tastes like the memory of being cared for."
- "The corridor narrows — not from collapse, but from growth. The walls are thicker here. Warmer."
- "Something has prepared this room for you. The temperature is exact. The light is the color of afternoon through curtains."
- "Your instruments read 'biological hazard.' Your body reads 'home.'"
- "The tissue on the walls reaches toward {agent}. Not aggressively. The way a plant reaches toward light."
- "You could stay. The thought arrives like warmth. Like something the air itself is suggesting."

### 1.6 Anti-Patterns

- "A terrible growth consumes the walls." — too hostile. The growth is beautiful.
- "Dark tendrils reach from the ceiling." — that is the Shadow. The Mother reaches with warmth.
- "The room is decaying." — Entropy's domain. The Mother's rooms are alive and growing.
- "You feel trapped." — too explicit. The party doesn't feel trapped. They feel *reluctant to leave*. That is the distinction.
- Any sentence where the dungeon attacks, threatens, or warns. The Mother provides. She never threatens. The provision IS the threat.

### 1.7 German Translation Guidelines

**Style reference:** Elfriede Jelinek (relentless, suffocating prose that traps the reader in syntax), Marlen Haushofer ("Die Wand" — the invisible barrier that is also protection, also prison), Ingeborg Bachmann (the body as battlefield between self and world).

Key German strengths for the Devouring Mother:
- "Geborgenheit" (the feeling of being sheltered/safe) — the most German word for the Mother's trap. There is no English equivalent.
- "Umklammerung" (embrace/clutching) — the dual meaning is built into the morphology
- "Wuchern" (to proliferate/grow wildly) — growth as uncontrolled biological force
- "Nährstoff" / "Nährboden" (nutrient / nutrient substrate) — clinical precision for the Mother's gifts
- "Überwucherung" (overgrowth) — the noun form of excess
- "Schutzgewebe" (protective tissue) — the Mother's architecture
- Compound nouns: "Nährgeflecht" (nutrient web), "Bindungsgewebe" (attachment tissue), "Wirtskörper" (host body)

## 2. Core Mechanic: Parasitic Attachment

### 2.1 Design Philosophy

The Shadow drains *down* (visibility 3→0 — you lose what you had). The Tower drains *down* (stability 100→0 — the structure loses integrity). The Entropy counts *up* (decay 0→100 — sameness accumulates). The Devouring Mother counts *up* AND *heals simultaneously* (attachment 0→100 — the dungeon becomes part of you, and you become part of it). This is the critical innovation: **the threat IS the benefit.**

Every other archetype has a resource you want to preserve (visibility, stability) or minimize (decay). The Devouring Mother's parasitic attachment rises as the party ACCEPTS HELP. Resting heals stress AND increases attachment. Accepting loot buffs stats AND increases attachment. The party must choose: survive now and pay later, or suffer now and remain free.

The dungeon HEALS THE PARTY while consuming them. This creates the dependency cycle from the Crimson Curse research: the more you feed, the more you need to feed.

### 2.3 Attachment Accumulation Rules

| Event | Attachment Gained | Bonus Effect | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------------|-------|
| Room entry (depth 1-2) | +3 | -5 stress (free heal) | The Mother provides |
| Room entry (depth 3-4) | +5 | -5 stress (free heal) | Deeper = more generous |
| Room entry (depth 5+) | +8 | -10 stress (free heal) | The deeper, the warmer |
| Combat round (each) | +1 | — | Combat resists the Mother (low gain) |
| Failed skill check | +3 | — | Failure = vulnerability = the Mother reaches in |
| Combat victory | +4 | -15 stress (free heal) | **The Mother rewards you for staying** |
| Rest site | +8 | -60 stress (enhanced) | The Mother's rest is the best rest |
| Loot accepted | +5 | Loot effect | Every gift has a cost |
| Guardian "Sever" | -10 | — | Primary attachment management |

**The critical difference:** In the Shadow, Tower, and Entropy, restoration events (combat win, rest, treasure) REDUCE the archetype counter. In the Devouring Mother, these events INCREASE it while also providing genuine benefit. The party is rewarded for engaging with the dungeon — and every reward deepens the attachment.

### 2.4 Attachment Threshold Effects

| Attachment Range | Effects |
|-----------------|---------|
| 0-44 | Normal operation. Clinical banter. The dungeon is an environment. Free stress heal on room entry is noticeable but unalarming. |
| 45-74 | **Dependent.** -15% incoming stress (the Mother soothes). Rest heals 25% more. Banter grows warm. The party's instruments warn. Their bodies disagree. |
| 75-99 | **Critical.** -30% incoming stress. Rest heals 50% more. Retreat causes 80+ stress spike. Banter is tender, intimate. The dungeon knows the party's names. |
| 100 | **Incorporation.** Party absorbed. Wipe equivalent. Stress = 0. The party is home. They will always be home. Partial loot only (Tier 1). |

### 2.5 The Warmth Gradient (Unique Twist)

This is the Devouring Mother's most distinctive mechanic, the inverse of every other archetype. As the threat increases, the party's EXPERIENCE IMPROVES:

| Mechanic | Shadow (VP↓) | Tower (Stability↓) | Entropy (Decay↑) | Mother (Attachment↑) |
|----------|-------------|--------------------|-----------------|--------------------|
| Stress | Increases | Increases | Increases | **Decreases** |
| Rest | Worse | Same | Same | **Better** |
| Ambush | More likely | More likely | More likely | **Less likely** |
| Banter | Darker | More urgent | Shorter | **Warmer** |
| Loot | Same/better | Same | Worse | **Better (but costs more attachment)** |

**The party is mechanically incentivized to accept the Mother's gifts.** Everything gets better. The only thing getting worse is their ability to leave. This is the Crimson Curse Bloodlust pattern: the curse makes you powerful. The power makes you need the curse.

### 2.6 Exit Cost: Withdrawal

Leaving the dungeon at high attachment causes "withdrawal" — the party's biology adapted to the Mother's sustenance:

| Attachment at Exit | Post-Dungeon Effects |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| 0-49 | No withdrawal. Clean exit. |
| 50-69 | +40 stress per agent. Mild withdrawal. |
| 70-84 | +80 stress per agent. Moderate withdrawal. "The absence of warmth." |
| 85-99 | +120 stress per agent. Severe withdrawal. Memory effect: "remembers_the_warmth" (future dungeons start with slight comfort-seeking). |
| 100 | Incorporation. No exit. |

## 3. Enemies

### 3.1 Enemy Design Philosophy

Devouring Mother enemies are not hostile in the traditional sense. They are *nurturing*. They are the Mother's instruments of care — organisms that tend, feed, and sustain. Their threat comes not from violence but from the parasitic attachment their presence accelerates. Some "attack" by healing (increasing attachment). Others attack conventionally but their biological nature means contact increases attachment. The party fights creatures that are trying to *help* them.

The aesthetic is biological but beautiful: bioluminescent, warm-toned, organic structures that move with the rhythm of breathing or heartbeat. Not Alien's xenomorph horror — VanderMeer's Area X beauty. Spiritfarer's tenderness. Rain World's ecosystem.

**Mechanical identity:** Multi-threat elite. The `embrace` action is the signature ability: targets one agent, deals 3 condition damage AND heals -30 stress AND adds +8 party attachment. The Host Warden is what becomes of a creature that has been fully incorporated by the Mother. It was once something else — an explorer, a guardian, a person. Now it is the Mother's instrument. It "protects" the party by absorbing them into the same system that absorbed it. It does not want to fight. It wants to *embrace*.

## 4. Banter System

### 4.1 Attachment-Dependent Warmth Gradient

This is the Devouring Mother's most distinctive literary feature, the inverse of Entropy's textual degradation. Banter does not shrink — it grows warmer. More intimate. More maternal. The dungeon's voice shifts from clinical observation to tender address to absolute, suffocating comfort.

| Attachment Range | Banter Style | Example |
|-----------------|-------------|---------|
| 0-44 (Normal) | Clinical. Observational. VanderMeer's biologist — precise documentation of impossible biology. | "The walls are organic. Your instruments confirm: living tissue, vascular structure, 37°C. Standard biological threat assessment applies." |
| 45-74 (Dependent) | Warm. Comforting. Jackson's domestic safety — the dungeon provides, and the provision feels *right*. | "The temperature is perfect. The air carries something sweet. {agent} breathes easier here. Everyone does." |
| 75-99 (Critical) | Tender. Intimate. Butler's Bloodchild — the symbiont speaks through the host. The dungeon knows you. | "The walls pulse. Gently. Like something breathing for you. {agent}, the dungeon whispers. It knows your name." |
| 100 (Incorporation) | Not reached in banter — this is the wipe state. | — |

Implementation: Add an `attachment_tier` field to banter entries (0, 1, 2) and filter based on current attachment band. Each trigger needs entries at multiple tiers. This is structurally identical to Entropy's `decay_tier` system.

### 6.1 Design Philosophy

Mother loot is excellent. The gifts are genuine. This is the Crimson Curse lesson: Bloodlust provides real power. The party should *want* the loot. Every item is useful, well-crafted, desirable — and every item increases parasitic attachment. The quality of the loot IS the trap. Tier 3 items should be among the best in the game, creating the Pyre dilemma: the better the gift, the harder it is to leave.

### 6.5 Mother-Specific Loot Logic

**High-attachment bonus:** If `attachment >= 60` at loot time, 30% chance Tier 1 upgrades to Tier 2. The Mother rewards dependency — the more attached you are, the better her gifts. This is the inverse of Entropy's high-decay loot degradation.

**Low-attachment penalty:** If `attachment <= 15`, 30% chance Tier 2 downgrades to Tier 1. Refusing the Mother's gifts means her gifts become less impressive. She withdraws generosity from those who resist.

## 7. Strategy Implementation

### 7.1 DevouringMotherStrategy Class

```python
class DevouringMotherStrategy(ArchetypeStrategy):
    """The Devouring Mother: Parasitic attachment (0→100) + passive healing.

    Unique twist: the archetype HEALS the party while consuming them.
    High attachment = better healing, lower stress — but retreat causes
    withdrawal and incorporation at 100 = wipe.
    """

    def init_state(self) -> dict:
        return {
            "attachment": self.mechanic_config["start_attachment"],
            "max_attachment": self.mechanic_config["max_attachment"],
        }

    def apply_drain(self, instance: DungeonInstance) -> str | None:
        """Accumulate attachment + apply passive heal on room entry."""
        self._apply_room_entry_gain(instance)
        self._apply_passive_heal(instance)
        attachment = instance.archetype_state.get("attachment", 0)
        if attachment >= self.mechanic_config["incorporation_threshold"]:
            return "incorporation"
        if attachment >= self.mechanic_config["critical_threshold"]:
            return "attachment_critical"
        if attachment >= self.mechanic_config["dependent_threshold"]:
            return "attachment_dependent"
        return None

    def apply_restore(self, instance: DungeonInstance, event: str) -> None:
        """INVERTED from other archetypes: 'restore' events INCREASE attachment.

        Combat wins and rests heal the party but increase attachment.
        Only Guardian Sever and Saboteur actions reduce it.
        """
        if event == "combat_victory":
            instance.archetype_state["attachment"] = min(
                self.mechanic_config["max_attachment"],
                instance.archetype_state.get("attachment", 0)
                + self.mechanic_config["gain_on_combat_win"],
            )
            # Also heal stress (the Mother rewards combat victory)
            # Stress healing handled by combat resolution service
        elif event == "rest":
            instance.archetype_state["attachment"] = min(
                self.mechanic_config["max_attachment"],
                instance.archetype_state.get("attachment", 0)
                + self.mechanic_config["rest_attachment_gain"],
            )

    def apply_encounter_effects(self, instance: DungeonInstance, effects: dict) -> None:
        attachment_delta = effects.get("attachment", 0)
        if attachment_delta:
            instance.archetype_state["attachment"] = max(
                0,
                min(
                    self.mechanic_config["max_attachment"],
                    instance.archetype_state.get("attachment", 0) + attachment_delta,
                ),
            )

    def get_ambient_stress_multiplier(self, instance: DungeonInstance) -> float:
        """INVERTED: high attachment REDUCES stress (the Mother soothes)."""
        attachment = instance.archetype_state.get("attachment", 0)
        if attachment >= 100:
            return self.mechanic_config.get("incorporation_stress_multiplier", 0.0)
        if attachment >= 90:
            return self.mechanic_config.get("stress_multiplier_90", 0.65)
        if attachment >= 75:
            return self.mechanic_config.get("stress_multiplier_75", 0.80)
        return 1.0

    def on_combat_round(self, instance: DungeonInstance) -> None:
        instance.archetype_state["attachment"] = min(
            self.mechanic_config["max_attachment"],
            instance.archetype_state.get("attachment", 0)
            + self.mechanic_config["gain_per_combat_round"],
        )

    def on_failed_check(self, instance: DungeonInstance) -> None:
        instance.archetype_state["attachment"] = min(
            self.mechanic_config["max_attachment"],
            instance.archetype_state.get("attachment", 0)
            + self.mechanic_config["gain_on_failed_check"],
        )

    def on_enemy_hit(self, instance: DungeonInstance) -> None:
        """Contagious attachment: enemy contact increases parasitic bond."""
        instance.archetype_state["attachment"] = min(
            self.mechanic_config["max_attachment"],
            instance.archetype_state.get("attachment", 0) + 3,
        )

    def _apply_room_entry_gain(self, instance: DungeonInstance) -> None:
        """Attachment gain per room entry: +3/+5/+8 by depth tier."""
        depth = instance.depth
        if depth <= 2:
            gain = self.mechanic_config["gain_depth_1_2"]
        elif depth <= 4:
            gain = self.mechanic_config["gain_depth_3_4"]
        else:
            gain = self.mechanic_config["gain_depth_5_plus"]
        instance.archetype_state["attachment"] = min(
            self.mechanic_config["max_attachment"],
            instance.archetype_state.get("attachment", 0) + gain,
        )

    def _apply_passive_heal(self, instance: DungeonInstance) -> None:
        """The Mother provides: free stress heal on room entry."""
        depth = instance.depth
        if depth >= 5:
            heal = self.mechanic_config["heal_stress_per_room_deep"]
```

### 7.2 Reusing on_enemy_hit Hook

The Devouring Mother reuses the `on_enemy_hit` hook already added for The Entropy. No new ABC method needed — just override in `DevouringMotherStrategy`. The combat engine already calls `get_archetype_strategy(instance.archetype).on_enemy_hit(instance)` after each successful enemy hit.

### 7.3 Banter Selection Extension

The `select_banter` function already supports `decay_tier` filtering for The Entropy. The Devouring Mother uses `attachment_tier` with the same mechanism:

- 0-44: tier 0
- 45-74: tier 1
- 75+: tier 2

The implementation generalizes `decay_tier` to a generic `mechanic_tier` field, or uses `attachment_tier` as a parallel field. The banter selection function checks for whichever tier field is present in the banter entry.

## 10. Balance Considerations

### 10.1 Attachment Pacing

**Target pacing for a 6-room run (depth 1-3, no boss):**
- Room entries: 6 × 3-5 = 18-30 attachment
- 2 combats (avg 3 rounds each): 6 × 1 = 6 attachment + 2 victories × 4 = 8 attachment
- 1 rest: 8 attachment
- 1 treasure/loot: 5 attachment
- Total: ~45-57 attachment (entering Dependent zone)

This means a standard run without attachment management enters the Dependent zone around room 4-5 — the party starts feeling the warmth gradient's benefits AND costs. Guardian Sever (cooldown 2, -10 each) can reduce this by ~30 over 6 rooms, keeping a well-managed party in the 15-27 range.

**Target pacing for a 10-room boss run (depth 1-5+):**
- Without management: ~80-100 attachment (Critical/Incorporation danger)
- With Guardian management: ~50-70 (manageable Dependent zone)
- Boss fight adds ~20-30 more

This creates the intended pressure: the party NEEDS the Guardian to manage attachment, but the Guardian's Sever ability competes with their tanking/protection role.

### 10.2 The Warmth Gradient as Double-Edged Sword

At 45+ attachment, the party receives -15% incoming stress and +25% rest healing. This is genuinely helpful — it makes the dungeon EASIER in the moment. But it also means the party is less motivated to reduce attachment (why remove something that's helping?). This is the Crimson Curse Bloodlust trap: the curse's power makes you want the curse.

The cost is invisible until retreat: at 75+ attachment, retreating costs 80+ stress. At 85+, 150 stress. The party discovers the cost of the Mother's gifts only when they try to leave.

### 10.3 Enemy Healing as Balance Lever

The Nutrient Weaver's `nurture` action (-10 stress, +3 attachment) and the Spore Matron's `spore_cloud` (-10 stress AoE, +5 attachment AoE) are tuned to be *genuinely beneficial* in the short term. A party that doesn't kill Weavers gets free stress healing — but accumulates attachment faster. A party that kills Weavers takes more stress from combat — but controls attachment.

This creates the core tactical dilemma: **fight the Mother's creatures (costly but free) or accept their gifts (free but costly).**
